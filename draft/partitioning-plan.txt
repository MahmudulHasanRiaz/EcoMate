# Database Partitioning Strategy (P28)

## 1. Requirement Analysis

### Why Partitioning?
As Fashionary scales, certain tables attract massive write and read volume, leading to:
- **Index Bloat**: Indexes become too large to fit in memory, slowing down random I/O.
- **Vacuum Multi-threading Issues**: Postgres AUTOVACUUM becomes less efficient on multi-million row tables.
- **Query Latency**: Scans on non-indexed ranges or complex aggregations (LedgerEntry/Order) degrade.
- **Maintenance**: Backups and restores take longer.

### Target Tables (Priority Order)
1.  **Order** (by `createdAt` / `date`) 
2.  **LedgerEntry** (by `date`)
3.  **OrderPaymentEvent** (by `paymentDate`)
4.  **InventoryMovement** (by `createdAt`)
5.  **Notification** (by `createdAt`)
6.  **WebhookFailure** (by `createdAt`)

## 2. Partitioning Strategy

### Scheme: RANGE Partitioning
We will use Native Postgres RANGE partitioning by month or year.
- **Window**: Monthly for high-volume logs (Notification, InventoryMovement).
- **Window**: Quarterly or Yearly for business records (Order, LedgerEntry).

### Implementation Approach (Phased)
1.  **Phase A**: Create new partitioned parent tables (e.g., `Order_New`).
2.  **Phase B**: Create partitions for historical years and 12 months into the future.
3.  **Phase C**: Migration window (Off-peak). Copy data from old to new.
4.  **Phase D**: Rename (Wrap in transaction). Swap `Order` -> `Order_Old`, `Order_New` -> `Order`.

## 3. Retention Policy

### Data Integrity
The policy is **Indefinite Retention**. No data will be deleted during this process.
- Older partitions can be moved to cheaper storage (Tablespaces) if needed.
- Cold data (e.g., Notifications > 1 year) can eventually be exported to S3/JSON but kept read-only in the database.

## 4. Query & Performance Impact

- **Constraint Exclusion**: The Postgres planner will exclude partitions that don't match the `WHERE` clause (e.g., `WHERE createdAt >= '2025-01-01'`).
- **Parallel Scans**: Partitions allow for better parallelization of large analytic queries.
- **Index Maintenance**: Indexes are local to each partition, keeping them compact and cacheable.

## 5. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lock Contention | High | Move data during low-traffic windows. Use `CONCURRENTLY` for index creation. |
| Application Breakage | High | Prisma requires specific configuration to work with native partitioning (using `@ignore` or views). |
| Data Drift | Medium | Use a transient trigger during the swap Phase to keep both tables in sync. |
| Disk Usage | Medium | Ensure 2x disk space available during the "Copy" phase. |

## 6. Rollback Plan
- Keep the original `Table_Old` for at least 30 days.
- In case of failure, swap the names back immediately.
